Fishman-Man Island

The Fish-Man Island Saga marks the Straw Hat Pirates’ long-awaited reunion after the two-year time skip. Beginning at episode 517, the saga shows each crew member returning stronger, more skilled, and fully prepared for the harsh seas of the New World. Once reunited at Sabaody Archipelago, they immediately clash with a redesigned Pacifista unit, easily defeating what once posed a major threat, proving their dramatic growth.

From there, the crew sets sail toward the deep sea, aiming to finally reach Fish-Man Island, a place hinted at since early One Piece. Their underwater voyage is filled with dangers like the Kraken, giant sea monsters, and deep-sea currents. Luffy tames the Kraken, symbolizing his new strength and leadership. After overcoming the descent, they finally enter the brilliantly glowing kingdom beneath the sea.

Upon arrival at Ryugu Palace, they meet King Neptune, Princess Shirahoshi, and the royal family. However, the island is in political turmoil. The saga focuses heavily on themes of racial discrimination, historical injustice, and the dream of coexistence between humans and fish-men. The story reveals the painful past of Queen Otohime and Fisher Tiger—two visionaries whose lives were dedicated to bringing peace between the races.

The main threat comes from Hody Jones, a fish-man extremist who despises humans. Fueled by drug-enhancing Energy Steroids, Hody aims to overthrow the royal family and plunge the island into war. Luffy and the Straw Hats step in to protect Fish-Man Island when Hody launches a full-scale takeover. The battle at Gyoncorde Plaza becomes one of the saga's climaxes, showing each Straw Hat demonstrating their improved abilities.

Luffy’s fight against Hody Jones is symbolic: Luffy fights not just an enemy but a cycle of inherited hatred. In a decisive showdown, he defeats Hody underwater, cementing his status as a protector of the island. Princess Shirahoshi is then revealed to be the ancient weapon Poseidon, a living ability capable of controlling Sea Kings, setting the groundwork for major future storylines.

At the end of the saga, Big Mom is introduced as the new powerful antagonist with a direct conflict against the Straw Hats, as Fish-Man Island becomes under her protection after Whitebeard's death. Luffy boldly tells Big Mom that he will defeat her one day, igniting a future emperor-level rivalry.

The saga ends with the Straw Hats finally leaving Fish-Man Island and ascending toward the New World, marking the true beginning of the second half of One Piece.

  • Return to Sabaody Arc: Episodes 517–522
  • Fish-Man Island Arc: Episodes 523–574
  • Toriko Crossover (Special): Episode 542
  • G-5 Arc (Filler): Episodes 575–578
