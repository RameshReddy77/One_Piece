Wano Country

The Wano Country Saga is one of the longest, most dramatic, and most visually stunning arcs in One Piece, telling the story of a samurai nation ruled under the iron grip of Kaido and the shogun Orochi. It is the climax of Luffy’s journey in the New World so far, marking his rise from a growing pirate to an emerging Emperor-level threat. The saga begins when the Straw Hat Pirates infiltrate the isolated land of Wano, a country inspired by feudal Japan, now suffering under tyranny, famine, and oppression. Luffy meets the samurai Kozuki Oden’s retainers, including Kin’emon, Raizo, Kanjuro, and later Denjiro, who all seek to restore Wano to its rightful heir—Kozuki Momonosuke.

The early part focuses on the Straw Hats blending into local life: Zoro becomes a ronin, Luffy is imprisoned in Udon after fighting Kaido, and Sanji secretly uses his Raid Suit for the first time. Meanwhile, alliances between the Straw Hats, the Heart Pirates, the Mink Tribe, and the samurai prepare for the Fire Festival Raid. The backstory of Kozuki Oden is revealed, showcasing his adventures with Whitebeard, his time with Gol D. Roger, and his tragic execution by Kaido and Orochi.

The Onigashima Raid forms the core of the saga, featuring massive battles across the floating island. The Red Scabbards challenge Kaido, while the Worst Generation—Luffy, Zoro, Law, Kid, and Killer—face the two Emperors, Kaido and Big Mom, on the rooftop. Zoro unleashes Conqueror’s Haki, Killer confronts Hawkins, Sanji battles Queen, and Jinbe clashes with Who’s-Who. Yamato, Kaido’s rebellious child, joins Luffy’s cause and protects Momonosuke. The raid intensifies when Luffy awakens Gear Fifth, transforming the final battle into one of the most spectacular moments in the series.

The saga concludes with the fall of Kaido and Big Mom, freeing Wano after 20 years of suffering. Momonosuke becomes the new shogun, the World Government declares Luffy one of the Four Emperors, and new mysteries unfold surrounding ancient weapons, Joy Boy, and the true history. The Straw Hats finally set sail again, changed forever by the war that reshaped the New World.
